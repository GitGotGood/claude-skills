# Severity and Fragility Rubric

Use this rubric to avoid vague prioritization.

## Fragility score (1-5)

### 1 — Low fragility
- strongly evidenced
- not central to the plan
- resilient if partly wrong

### 2 — Mild fragility
- somewhat evidenced
- useful but not load-bearing
- fallback paths already exist

### 3 — Moderate fragility
- partly inferred
- important to expected success
- failure would force meaningful revision

### 4 — High fragility
- weakly evidenced or context-sensitive
- central to economics, adoption, or execution
- failure would likely damage the plan materially

### 5 — Critical fragility
- largely assumed
- highly load-bearing
- if false, the current plan is likely invalid or badly misframed

## Evidence labels
- **Observed:** direct evidence or demonstrated behavior
- **Inferred:** reasonable conclusion from partial evidence
- **Guessed:** plausible but not grounded well
- **Inherited:** accepted from precedent or default belief more than proof

## Recommendation priority
Prioritize issues that are:
1. high fragility
2. high impact if false
3. easy or cheap to test
4. central to the decision now
